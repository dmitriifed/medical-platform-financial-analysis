# Key Findings

## 1. Revenue Growth Without Margin Growth
- Revenue increases over time
- Gross margin declines
- Unit economics weaken with scale
- Growth driven by volume, not efficiency

## 2. Video Consultations Drive Profitability
- Higher median gross profit per consultation
- Higher total gross profit
- Stronger unit economics
- Primary growth lever

## 3. Chat Consultations Are Low Margin
- Lower profit per consultation
- Dilutes overall margin
- High volume but weak profitability

## 4. Rising Cancellations Reduce Revenue
- Lost revenue increases over time
- Scheduling inefficiencies
- Supply-demand mismatch
- Unused doctor capacity

## 5. Revenue Concentration Risk
- Small group of doctors generates majority of revenue
- High dependency on top providers
- Limited scalability
- Operational risk

## 6. Most Profitable Specialties
Top gross profit contributors:
- Endocrinology & Nutrition
- General Medicine
- Surgery & Oncology
- Cardio & Internal Medicine

## 7. Weak Patient Retention
- Cohort retention declines
- Limited repeat consultations
- Low lifetime value
- Reliance on new users

## 8. Pareto Distribution in Both Doctors and Patients
- Small share drives majority of revenue
- Concentration risk
- Opportunity for diversification
