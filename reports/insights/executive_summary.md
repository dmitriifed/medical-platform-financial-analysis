# Executive Summary — Medical Consultation Platform

The platform demonstrates strong growth in consultation volume and revenue, but profitability is not scaling at the same pace. While revenue increases steadily, gross margin declines over time, indicating weakening unit economics and operational inefficiencies. The business is expanding, but efficiency is deteriorating.

Service-level analysis shows that video consultations generate significantly higher gross profit than chat consultations. Video contributes both higher margin and higher total gross profit, while chat remains a lower-margin product. This creates a structural imbalance in the service mix, where growth is partially driven by less profitable interactions.

Operational performance is impacted by rising cancellations. Lost revenue from cancellations increases over time, representing a major source of inefficiency. These cancellations reduce monetization, waste acquisition costs, and create unused doctor capacity.

Revenue is highly concentrated among a small group of doctors. A limited share of providers generates the majority of revenue, creating dependency risk and limiting scalability. Expanding the active doctor base is necessary for sustainable growth.

Specialty-level performance shows that endocrinology, general medicine, and surgery-related categories generate the highest gross profit. These specialties represent the strongest opportunities for scaling revenue with healthy unit economics.

Patient behavior indicates limited long-term retention. Many patients do not return frequently after the first consultation, which constrains lifetime value and increases reliance on continuous acquisition.

Overall, the platform is successfully growing demand, but improvements in service mix, operational efficiency, and retention are required to transition from volume-driven growth to sustainable profitability.
